B

âœ… CORRECT ANSWER: C

Why this is correct: List hygiene must be the first priority when facing a 15% bounce rate, which is catastrophically high compared to the industry standard of under 2%. Hard bounces directly damage sender reputation with Internet Service Providers (ISPs) and email clients. When ISPs see high bounce rates, they interpret this as poor list management practices and begin filtering emails to spam folders or blocking them entirely. Cleaning the list immediately removes invalid email addresses, non-existent domains, and role accounts that generate bounces, providing an instant improvement to deliverability metrics and sender reputation. This foundational step creates a clean foundation for all other email marketing efforts.

Why other options are incorrect:

  • Option A: Re-engagement campaigns are valuable but addressing them before list hygiene is premature. Sending re-engagement emails to invalid addresses will continue generating bounces and further damage sender reputation. Re-engagement should be implemented after establishing proper list hygiene practices.
  • Option B: Increasing email frequency when facing deliverability issues will exponentially worsen the problem. More emails sent to invalid addresses means more bounces, leading to faster reputation degradation and potential blacklisting by ISPs. This approach directly contradicts email marketing best practices.
  • Option D: Switching IP addresses is a costly and temporary solution that doesn't address the underlying data quality issues. The new IP will quickly inherit the same reputation problems when the same poor-quality list generates bounces and complaints. IP reputation follows sending practices, not the reverse.

Key Learning Points:

  • Industry standard bounce rates should remain below 2% for optimal deliverability
  • List hygiene is the foundation of all email marketing success and must precede other optimization efforts
